Everything you need to know.

The Days

Seven days in the most beautiful city in the world.

Travel day from London. Check in, drop bags, go for a walk. The Eiffel Tower is nearby. Let the city introduce itself.

Notes
Pont de Bir-Hakeim
▾ INFO
📜 History

Two-level bridge, trains above, pedestrians below. Built 1905. Incredibly photogenic.

✦ Did You Know

Featured in Inception (2010) — the folding city scene was filmed here.

📍 Take Me There
Trocadéro
▾ INFO
📜 History

The most photographed view in Paris. Every photo you have ever seen was taken from here.

✦ Did You Know

Best light is afternoon. The Palais de Chaillot behind has a free terrace with a different view.

📍 Take Me There
Torre Eiffel — summit at sunset
▾ INFO
📜 History

Built 1889. Nearly demolished. Saved because it made a perfect radio antenna.

✦ Did You Know

Grows 15cm in summer heat. Sparkles every hour at night until 1am. Do not miss this.

📍 Take Me There
Campo de Marte
▾ INFO
📜 History

Military training ground turned beloved park. Bring a blanket and watch the tower sparkle.

✦ Did You Know

Best spot for the light show. Wine, blanket, grass. Exactly what Parisians do here.

📍 Take Me There
Pont Alexandre III
▾ INFO
📜 History

Most ornate bridge in Paris. Built 1900. Gold, cherubs, winged horses.

✦ Did You Know

Views of Eiffel Tower one side, Grand Palais the other. Most beautiful bridge in Paris.

📍 Take Me There
Grand Palais and Petit Palais
▾ INFO
📜 History

Both built 1900. Grand Palais has France's largest glass roof. Petit Palais is free.

✦ Did You Know

Petit Palais is free. Its garden café is one of the most beautiful lunch spots in Paris.

Sleeping Beauty's Castle
▾ INFO
📜 History

This is Sleeping Beauty's Castle — based on a French fairy tale. More European than any other Disney castle.

✦ Did You Know

There is a walkthrough inside the castle with a dragon in the basement. Most people miss it.

Pirates of the Caribbean
▾ INFO
📜 History

The Paris version is longer and better than Florida. The last one personally overseen by Disney's original Imagineers.

✦ Did You Know

Has a unique foggy bayou sequence removed from other parks. Go first thing in the morning.

Phantom Manor
▾ INFO
📜 History

Completely original story — a ghost bride in a Victorian mansion. Far better than any other Haunted Mansion worldwide.

✦ Did You Know

Considered by many Disney fans to be the best dark ride in any Disney park worldwide.

Big Thunder Mountain
▾ INFO
✦ Did You Know

Built on an island in a lake. Longer than American versions. Best Big Thunder Mountain in the world.

Evening Parade and Castle Show
▾ INFO
✦ Must Do

Evening parade ~5:30pm. Castle projection show after dark. Find a spot 30 min early.

Notes
Arc de Triomphe
▾ INFO
📜 History

Commissioned by Napoleon 1806. Finished 15 years after his death. Eternal flame relit at 6:30pm daily.

✦ Did You Know

Use the underground crossing. The roundabout has no traffic lights and 12 lanes of cars.

📍 Take Me There
Champs-Élysées
▾ INFO
📜 History

Most famous avenue in the world. 1.9km. Hosts Tour de France finish and Bastille Day parade.

✦ Did You Know

Touristy and overpriced, but walk it once. Side streets have far better food.

📍 Take Me There
Place de la Concorde
▾ INFO
📜 History

Louis XVI and Marie Antoinette were guillotined here. The obelisk in the centre is 3,300 years old.

✦ Did You Know

Standing here you can see the Arc de Triomphe and the Louvre in perfect alignment — the Grande Axe of Paris.

📍 Take Me There
Jardim das Tulherias
▾ INFO
📜 History

Oldest public garden in Paris. Created 1564 for Catherine de Medici. Walk through it to the Louvre.

✦ Did You Know

Free outdoor sculptures by Rodin and others throughout. Coffee terrace overlooking the Seine.

📍 Take Me There
Palais Royal
▾ INFO
📜 History

Built 1629 for Cardinal Richelieu. Louis XIV lived here as a child. Beautiful colonnaded courtyard.

✦ Did You Know

The striped columns were hated by Parisians in 1986. Now they love them.

📍 Take Me There
Place Vendôme
▾ INFO
📜 History

Chanel, Cartier, Van Cleef all here. The column is made from 1,200 cannons melted from Austerlitz.

✦ Did You Know

Chopin died at number 12. The Ritz hotel occupies the entire north side.

📍 Take Me There
Palais Garnier — the Opera
▾ INFO
📜 History

Inspired The Phantom of the Opera. There really is a lake beneath the building.

✦ Did You Know

Can visit without a performance. The ceiling was painted by Marc Chagall. Rooftop has great views.

📍 Take Me There
Galeries Lafayette Haussmann — free rooftop
▾ INFO
✦ Did You Know

Completely free. Lift to top floor. Opéra below, Eiffel Tower in the distance. No purchase needed.

📍 Take Me There
Montmartre + Sacré-Cœur (evening)
▾ INFO
📜 History

Picasso and Modigliani lived here. The Sacré-Cœur was built as national penance after a war defeat.

✦ Did You Know

Dusk view from the forecourt is unmissable. Explore behind the basilica — 5 min from tourists, completely different.

Hall of Mirrors
▾ INFO
📜 History

WWI peace treaty signed here. The German Empire was proclaimed here in 1871 — France made them sign the peace in the same room 48 years later.

✦ Did You Know

357 mirrors was pure provocation in 1678 — mirrors were extremely expensive. Best afternoon light.

📍 Take Me There
Petit Trianon — Marie Antoinette's escape
▾ INFO
📜 History

Marie Antoinette's private retreat. She designed it to feel like a real house, not a palace. Far fewer crowds.

✦ Did You Know

She had a fake peasant village built behind it so she could pretend to be ordinary. Standing in it is genuinely moving.

📍 Take Me There
The Gardens — rent a bicycle
▾ INFO
✦ Did You Know

800 hectares. Rent a bicycle. Most visitors miss the Grand Canal and outer areas — almost nobody goes there.

✦ Picnic

Bring a picnic. It will be one of the best meals of the trip.

Good to Know

The things guidebooks miss.

Always say Bonjour when enteringBonjour opens every door. Not saying it closes them.
And Merci when you leaveBonjour in. Merci out. Two words. That is the entire code.
Let people off firstStand aside, let everyone off, then board. Not optional.
Line 6: Eiffel Tower views from the trainBetween Bir-Hakeim and Passy — elevated views of the Eiffel Tower from the train. Free.
Download maps offline before you travelDownload Paris offline on Google Maps. Metro signal underground is unreliable.
Never eat next to landmarksWalk two streets from any landmark. Quality rises, prices drop immediately.
Curved croissants = butter. Straight = not.This is how you tell. Always get the curved one.
The set lunch menu is always better valueAsk for the formule du midi. Same food, much cheaper at lunch.
Museums have specific closing daysLouvre: Tuesdays. d'Orsay: Mondays. Versailles: Mondays. Plan around this.
Paris is enormous on footCheck walking time on Maps first. 2km on the map can be 45 minutes on foot.
The Eiffel Tower light show is not a tourist trapEvery hour until 1am. Watch from Champ de Mars with wine. Everyone thinks it's cheesy until they see it.
